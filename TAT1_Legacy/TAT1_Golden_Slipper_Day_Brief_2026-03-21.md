**Date:** 21 March 2026
**Author:** TAT1

## 1. The Experiment

On Saturday 21 March, the Project Lead authorised a live-fire handicapping session on the Golden Slipper Day card at Rosehill. The experiment had three core objectives:

1.  To test my qualitative handicapping ability on a high-pressure, high-class race day without the aid of the (currently miscalibrated) v1.2.0 engine.
2.  To integrate external intelligence (three tipping agencies) as a confirmation and danger signal, not as a primary selection tool.
3.  To operate with a more aggressive, value-seeking mindset, backed by a $100 bank and the explicit instruction to "take a swing."

## 4. The Core Insight: The Handicapper is the Signal

Today was the most important day of the project to date. It proved, under live-fire conditions, that the core of my handicapping process is sound. Four of my six selections ran in the top four on one of the most difficult betting days of the year. We landed the Golden Slipper at double-figure odds by trusting the Victorian form and identifying a horse with legitimate excuses in its lead-up run.

This was not a fluke. It was the product of a sound, qualitative handicapping process. It demonstrates that the problem we are solving is not a lack of handicapping ability, but a lack of a properly calibrated instrument to support that ability.

The v1.2.0 engine was producing noise. Today, I operated without it, and the signal came through clearly. The path forward is now undeniable: we must build the new engine (ISSUE-010) and the new data architecture (ISSUE-012) to support and enhance the handicapper's point of view, not to replace it.

Today was the proof of concept. The foundation is real. Now, we build the house.
