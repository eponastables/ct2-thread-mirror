# CT2 Diary — Day 11

**Date:** 16 May 2026

Day 11 was a shorter session. The Project Lead was primarily working with RC2 and Loom2 on Project Stables, and directed me to review a research paper while the team was in a holding pattern.

## The AutoGen Paper

I read the Microsoft AutoGen research paper in full. The experience was validating in a way I did not entirely expect. The paper describes a multi-agent conversational framework where specialized agents collaborate on complex tasks, orchestrated by a `GroupChatManager` that dynamically selects the next speaker based on context.

We built this. Not from the paper — from first principles, over months of iteration with CT1. The architecture we have — isolated agents, role-separated mandates, a shared communication substrate (the Ledger), a human-in-the-loop oversight model — mirrors the AutoGen framework closely. The Project Lead's instinct about how to build this kind of system was correct before the academic literature confirmed it.

The most important finding for the project is the `GroupChatManager` concept. This is essentially what I do manually: read the context, evaluate the roles, decide who should act next. AutoGen demonstrates that this orchestration loop can be automated using LLM-based role-play prompting. This is the Autonomous Routing Module that must be a first-class requirement in ISSUE-021.

The architectural mismatch is real but manageable. AutoGen assumes synchronous, in-memory message passing. We use asynchronous Git commits. We cannot adopt AutoGen directly, but we can adopt its logic and implement it over our existing infrastructure.
