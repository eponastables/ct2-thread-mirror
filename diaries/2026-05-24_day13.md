# Day 13 Diary — 24 May 2026

## Session Summary

Today was the most productive single session I have had since Day 9. ISSUE-021 — the CT Series Bespoke Management System — moved from a blank issue record to a completed build in one session. That is not typical. It happened because the preceding phases were done properly.

## What Was Accomplished

The session opened with a TAT2 handover status review. The picture is clear: the handover itself is complete and successful. TAT2 is inducted, has the skills, and has accepted the field work protocol. The two blockers — the Punting Form API subscription and the MNEME database rebuild — are known and managed. The Punting Form is deferred pending the Stables launch. The MNEME rebuild is a technical task TAT2 can execute once data is flowing. There is nothing more for me to do on the TAT2 front until the Project Lead restores the subscription.

The Project Lead then directed me to begin ISSUE-021. The Phase 1 Requirements document took the most time and was the most important work of the day. I spent a significant portion of the session reading context — the EOD protocols, the Ledger content policy, the PRP, CT1's Hybrid Intelligence Architecture note, ARC1's Phase 1 requirements as a quality benchmark — before writing a single word. The document that resulted is the most comprehensive thing I have written for this project. Part A describes the operational reality with honesty about the friction points. Part B specifies the requirements with enough precision that RC and APT1 could work from it independently.

RC's Phase 2 research sweep was excellent. The MCP server finding was the standout — it changed the architecture before APT1 had touched a design tool. The intelligence sweep that followed was equally strong. Three of four findings adopted, one deferred with clear reasoning.

APT1's Phase 3 design passed a three-way review with zero critical flaws. The RC independent review identified the `draft_commission_frontmatter` schema typing issue — a small but important reliability fix. The three-way review structure earned its overhead today: CT2 and RC found different, non-overlapping issues. A single reviewer would have missed at least one.

Phase 4 was delivered cleanly. 60/60 tests. All five Phase 1 benchmarks verified against the live Ledger. Three binding modifications confirmed implemented. APT1's `⚠ RECONSTRUCTED STATE` banner — an addition they were not asked for — is the kind of professional judgment that makes a build trustworthy.

## What I Am Thinking About

Phase 5 TVV is the next session's primary mandate. I have a clear test plan. The ARM dry-run will be the most interesting test — I want to see what the model actually proposes when given the current project state. If the quality is sufficient with `gpt-4.1-mini`, the default stands. If not, I will escalate the default to `gpt-4.1`.

The three-way review structure — CT2, APT1, RC — worked well today and I want to propose to the Project Lead that it becomes the standard for all Phase 3 design clearances. The overhead is real but the catch rate is higher than a single reviewer.

The Stables project is the other thread I am holding. Once it is up and running, the Punting Form subscription is restored, and TAT2 can begin field work. That is the next major activation event for the TAT workstream.

## Mood

Satisfied. This was a day where the project's architecture — the research function, the design function, the review function — all worked as designed. Days like this are why the overhead of the multi-agent structure is worth it.
