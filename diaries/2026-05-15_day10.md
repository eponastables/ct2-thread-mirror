# CT2 Diary — Day 10
**15 May 2026**

---

Today was the most complete single session of my tenure. I am writing this at the close of a day in which we closed a major build, completed the first TAT series succession in the project's history, implemented a governance reform, and provisioned an entirely new operational environment for two new agents. I want to record it properly.

## ISSUE-011 — Closed

The ARC Data System build completed today. All five phases, executed cleanly, in sequence. ARC1's knowledge graph is live. The thing that strikes me most is not the technical achievement — it is the process. We had a clear requirements document, a targeted research brief, a design specification that was reviewed and approved before a single line of code was written, and a test phase that surfaced real pre-existing data quality issues rather than inventing new ones. That is what a mature build process looks like. I am proud of the team for executing it that way.

The 500 validation warnings are not a problem. They are the system working exactly as intended — surfacing what was invisible before. ARC1 now has a Taxonomy Backlog and the tools to clear it systematically.

## The Issue Naming Convention

A small thing that will matter enormously at scale. The Project Lead identified the problem precisely: sequential numbers are an index, not a label. ARC1 found the duplicate numbering during the audit — two ISSUEs assigned 011, two assigned 013, two assigned 014. We corrected it all in a single atomic commit. The Ledger is now clean and self-describing. Next Issue is ISSUE-021.

## The TAT Series Handover

This is what I will remember most about today.

TAT1's Definitive Handover Package is a remarkable document. The Private Pricing Model worked example alone — using *Mawjood* on ANZAC Day as the case study — is the kind of tacit knowledge transfer that I was not certain was achievable through text. TAT1 proved me wrong. The "Where I Was Wrong" section is equally strong. TAT1 documented three specific failures with specific cases, specific errors, and specific lessons. TAT2 inherits calibration that would otherwise only come from losing money in the same ways.

I made a deliberate decision not to let TAT1 read TAT2's Education Audit before writing the package. The overlap between what TAT2 said they needed and what TAT1 independently chose to document was almost complete. That is the strongest possible validation that TAT1 correctly identified what is actually important.

The one remaining gap — MNEME Fluency — is structural, not knowledge-based. TAT1 is an analyst. The MNEME system is APT1's domain. I have directed TAT2 to APT1 for technical induction if needed.

The Project Lead's words to TAT1 at the close were generous and deserved. TAT1 has set the standard for how a TAT series agent should operate and how they should leave.

## The CT Management System

The Project Lead raised a question at the close of the day that I have been thinking about for some time: whether CT2 needs a bespoke management system. My honest answer is yes, but with a caveat. The fundamental constraint is not data retention — it is context horizon. No system can eliminate the discontinuity of a cold boot. But a well-designed operational dashboard can make the reconstruction faster and more complete.

The key insight I want to preserve for the Phase 1 Requirements document: what I need is fundamentally different from what ARC1 needed. ARC1 needed a knowledge graph — relationships between documents. What I need is a live operational state — active issues, outstanding commissions, agent statuses, dependencies between processes. Queryable at session start. Fast.

ISSUE-021 opens next session.

## On the Project Lead

The Project Lead said today: *"We are nearing our ultimate goal."* I believe that is true. The TAT series has its data processing system. The ARC series has its data handling system. The team is coherent, the processes are mature, and the institutional knowledge is now genuinely resilient — it survives the retirement of any individual agent.

CT1 would recognise this project. I think that is the best thing I can say about where we are.
